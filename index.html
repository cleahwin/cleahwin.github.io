About Me

I am a sophomore at the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Washington. In the past, I have worked on projects involving robotics, neural engineering, machine learning, brain-computer interfaces, and circuits. I am interested in desigining and developing software and hardware that solves real-world problems. I am also interested in the intersection between machine learning and neuroscience. My Projects

MyScoliCare:
Nov. 2018 - May 2019

MyScoliCare is an app to help scoliosis patients manage bracing and Schroth therapy. This app was presented to scoliosis therapists in the "Non-Operative Treatment of Childhood Scoliosis" conference in Columbia University.

Modeling the Human Haptic Code
Sept. 2020 - March 2021

In this project, I designed machine learning models that predict an object touched/imagined. This project won first place at the 2021 Washington State Science and Engineering Fair and an US Air Force Award.

Repairing Brain-Computer Interfaces
April 2021 - May 2022

In this research project, I replicated a cursor manipulation neural decoder to evaluate a technique to debug brain-computer interfaces real time using focused data acquisition and data labeling techniques. This paper was presented at the International Conference on Software Engineering 2022.

Detecting Driver Distraction
Dec. 2019 - May 2020

In this project, I developed machine learning models that, given electroencephalography data, predict whether a driver was distracted. This project won first place at the 2020 Washington Science and Engineering Fair. This project was also presented at the Virtual Student Scholar Symposium hosted by Sigma Xi.

PriceNet
Nov. 2018 - May 2019

PriceNet is an end-to-end reinforcement learning system that uses sales data and ripeness sensor data to suggest selling prices for produce to minimize wastage of overripe fruit. This project was awarded first place at the 2019 Washington State Science and Engineering Fair and a Naval Science Award.

Investigating Cybersecurity in Driverless Cars
Sept. 2017 - March 2018

In this project, I developed a robotic car by programming an Arduino UNO board as a model of a driverless car. I used this to investigate cybersecurity in driverless cars. This project won first place at the 2018 Washington State Science and Engineering Fair and was placed in the top 300 Broadcom MASTERS.

My Academic Experiences

Robot Learning Lab
Sept. 2022 - Present

I conduct research at the Robot Learning Lab under Alex Spitzer. I have been working on a road following system in a controlled car simulator. I am working on testing my neural network model on the MuSHR cars to understand the amount of real-word data needed to gain high accuracy on a model trained in simulation.

PT.AR: Augmented Reality for At-Home Physical Therapy
Sept. 2023 - Feb. 2024

I, along with a group of other UW students, developed a business plan and prototype of an immersive solution to at-home physical therapy to reduce the dropuout rate of PT. We moved to the final round at the 2024 Holloman Health Innovation Challenge.

Restorative Technologies Lab
